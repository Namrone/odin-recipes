Chicken Soup

A spin on normal chicken soup by adding tomatoes into the mix

This chicken soup recipe puts a little more soul into it. You create your own chicken stock and add tomato puree and paste for a bolder flavor. The noodles/carbs is up to your choice but included is a dumpling recipe.

Ingredients

For Chicken Stock

  • 2 chicken carcasses or 2 pounds chicken wings
  • 1 medium carrot, roughly chopped
  • 1 medium onion, roughly chopped
  • 1 rib celery, roughly chopped
  • 3-4 cloves garlic
  • 1 sprig thyme
  • 2 sprigs parsley
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 tablespoons black peppercorns

For Soup

  • 1 whole chicken or 5 chicken thighs
  • 2-3 tbs chicken fat
  • 2 cloves garlic
  • 1 medium onion
  • 1 medium carrot
  • 2 ribs celery
  • 1 cup crushed tomatoes
  • 2 yukon gold or similar waxy potatoes
  • 1 sprig thyme
  • 3 sprigs parsley
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1/2 pound orzo or rice
  • 1/4 cup pecorino romano, grated
  • Salt
  • Black pepper

For Dumpling

  • 2 cups flour
  • 1 1/2 teaspoon baking powder
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil or melted chicken fat
  • 1 cup water or chicken stock
  • 1/2 cup pecorino, grated
  • 1 egg
  • Salt
  • Black pepper

Steps

For Stock

  1. Roast the chicken at 350 F until golden brown.
  2. Add to a stockpot and cover with cold water. Make sure to deglaze any brown bits from the bottom of the roasting pan and add to the pot.
  3. Bring to a simmer and skim any fat and scum the top. Let simmer for 1 1/2-2 hours.
  4. Strain and reserve.
  5. Skim off chicken fat for use in the final recipe. Ice down if you are going to use it later.

For Soup

  1. Season the thighs and roast at 350 F until brown.
  2. Heat the chicken fat in a stockpot on medium.
  3. Add the onion and garlic. Sweat until soft.
  4. Add the carrots and celery. Sweat for 3-5 minutes.
  5. Add the roasted chicken and all juice to the pot. Cover with the reserved stock.
  6. Add the tomatoes and bring to a simmer.
  7. Tie the herbs into a bouquet garni and add. Let the soup simmer until the chicken is tender.
  8. Remove the chicken and let cool. Once cool remove the skin and bones and shred.
  9. Add the potatoes and simmer for 15 minutes.
  10. Add the dumplings and cook for 10 minutes.
  11. Remove the bouquet. Season with salt and pepper.
  12. Serve with the shredded chicken, chopped parsley and pecorino.

For Dumpling

  1. Combine all of the dry ingredients.
  2. Combine egg, olive oil and chicken fat. Add dry ingredients and mix to form a soft dough.
  3. Using a teaspoon, drop into the soup.
